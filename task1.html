What is FCFS Scheduling?

The First-Come, First-Served (FCFS) algorithm is the simplest type of CPU scheduling. As the name suggests, the process that requests the CPU first is allocated the CPU first. It is a non-preemptive algorithm, meaning once a process starts executing, it holds the CPU until it either terminates or performs an I/O operation.

Key Concept: Think of it like a queue at a grocery store. The person who gets in line first is served first, regardless of how many items they have.

The Convoy Effect

One major disadvantage of FCFS is the Convoy Effect. This happens when a CPU-intensive process (long burst time) arrives before several shorter processes. The short processes must wait a long time for the large process to finish, leading to high average waiting times and poor resource utilization.
