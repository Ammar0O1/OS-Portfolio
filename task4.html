Idea of the Algorithm

In priority scheduling, the operating system gives every process a priority value. When the CPU becomes free, it looks at the processes that already arrived and selects the one with the best priority. In this page, priority number 1 is the strongest, then 2, then 3, and so on.

Non-preemptive rule: after a process gets the CPU, it continues until its burst time finishes. It will not be interrupted in the middle.

Simple Notes

  • Ready queue: processes that arrived and are waiting for the CPU.
  • Waiting time: start time minus arrival time.
  • Turnaround time: completion time minus arrival time.
  • Problem: weak priority processes can wait for a long time. This is called starvation.
  • Possible solution: aging, where waiting processes slowly get better priority.
